# StreamCore Revenue Analytics

Revenue analysis project for a fictional SaaS company (StreamCore). Looks at why monthly recurring revenue growth slowed down over the last two quarters, and what to do about it.

## Business Context

StreamCore is a subscription streaming service with four plans (Basic, Standard, Premium, Family), customers across 10 countries, split into Consumer, SMB and Enterprise segments. Revenue kept growing through 2023-2025, but the growth rate dropped from about 4.2% per month in the first half of 2025 to about 2.2% in the second half. Leadership wants to know what's driving the slowdown and whether it needs a fix.

## Business Questions

- Is the slowdown coming from fewer new customers, or from existing customers leaving faster?
- Which plan or segment is driving the change in churn?
- Is this a regional issue or something specific to the product/pricing?
- Are newer customer cohorts retaining worse than older ones?
- Which customers and plans generate the most lifetime value, and where should acquisition focus shift?
- What does the growth trend look like over the next two quarters if nothing changes?

## Dataset

The dataset is synthetic - generated with a Python script, not real company data - but built to behave like a real SaaS business: signups grow with seasonality, churn depends on plan and customer tenure, customers occasionally upgrade or downgrade plans.

- ~106,000 customers
- ~1.1 million subscription-month records
- 36 months of history (Jan 2023 - Dec 2025)

Files: `data/customers.csv`, `data/subscriptions_monthly.csv`. Generated by `data/generate_data.py`.

## Data Model

Two tables, related by `customer_id`:

**customers**
| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| customer_id | unique customer identifier |
| signup_date | date customer signed up |
| signup_cohort | signup month, used for cohort analysis |
| country | customer's country |
| initial_plan | plan at signup |
| segment | Consumer / SMB / Enterprise |

**subscriptions_monthly**
| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| customer_id | links to customers table |
| month / month_date | the month of this record |
| plan | plan the customer was on that month |
| mrr | monthly recurring revenue from this customer that month |
| tenure_months | months since signup |
| is_churned_this_month | whether the customer churned that month |

One row per customer per active month. A customer stops appearing after the month they churn.

## SQL Analysis

Queries in `sql/queries.sql` (SQLite), covering:

- Monthly MRR, ARR, ARPU and growth rate
- Revenue by plan, country, segment
- MRR movement: new / expansion / contraction / churn

## Recommendations

The slowdown is a churn problem, not an acquisition problem. New signups held up fine through H2 2025. The drop in growth traces back almost entirely to churn going from 3.84% to 4.37% per month, concentrated in the Basic plan (5.65% to 6.58%) and the Consumer segment (4.29% to 4.89%). Enterprise churn barely moved. Country-level churn rose evenly everywhere, which rules out a regional cause. Cohort retention held steady across signup months, so this is hitting the existing customer base, not new signups.

Basic/Consumer is also the lowest-LTV group ($70 average vs $312 for Enterprise+Family), so the segment driving the most churn is also the least profitable one - but it's 45% of the customer base by volume, so it still drags down the aggregate numbers.

1. Look into why Basic-plan churn specifically increased in H2 2025 (price change, competitor offer, feature change)
2. Targeted retention offer for Consumer/Basic near renewal, not a blanket discount
3. Shift some acquisition effort toward Family/Premium plans - lower churn, 3-4x the LTV of Basic
4. Keep investing in Enterprise/SMB - low churn, highest LTV, currently only 30% of the base combined
5. Track churn by plan going forward, not just company-wide - the aggregate number hid a much sharper issue in one segment

Full breakdown in `insights.md`.
